MORNING BREW — MARKETS EDITION
Friday, April 18, 2026

EARNINGS ROUNDUP: BIG TECH DELIVERS

It was a strong night for mega-cap earnings. Microsoft (MSFT) reported Q3 results after the bell Thursday, posting EPS of $3.46 versus the $3.22 consensus estimate — a 7.5% beat. Azure cloud revenue grew 31% year-over-year, ahead of the 28% street expectation. Shares rose 4.2% in after-hours trading.

Netflix (NFLX) also topped estimates, reporting 5.1 million net subscriber additions versus the 4.2 million expected. Revenue came in at $10.5 billion, above the $10.2 billion consensus. The company raised full-year guidance. Shares jumped 6.8% after hours.

Amazon (AMZN) beat on the bottom line with EPS of $1.59 vs $1.36 expected, driven by AWS margin expansion. However, revenue guidance for Q2 came in slightly below consensus, capping gains to around 2% in extended trading.

On the miss side, Intel (INTC) disappointed with a revenue miss and a weak forward outlook citing slower-than-expected PC refresh cycle. Shares fell 8% after hours.

MACRO PICTURE

The broader market closed slightly higher Thursday ahead of the earnings wave. The S&P 500 gained 0.4%, the Nasdaq added 0.6%. The 10-year Treasury yield held steady at 4.32%.

Fed Governor Waller spoke Thursday afternoon, reiterating the data-dependent stance and noting that recent inflation prints have been "encouraging but not yet sufficient" to justify rate cuts.

SECTOR WATCH

Technology outperformed Thursday, with the XLK ETF up 0.8%. Energy pulled back 0.6% on softer crude prices. Financials were flat ahead of next week's bank earnings.

The AI infrastructure theme continued to dominate — Nvidia (NVDA) hit a new all-time high intraday before closing up 1.9%.
