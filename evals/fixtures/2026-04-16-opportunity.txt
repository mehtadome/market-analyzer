STOCKTWITS DAILY RIP
Wednesday, April 16, 2026

FED PIVOTS — RATE CUT BACK ON THE TABLE

In a surprise move, Fed Chair Powell signaled Wednesday that the FOMC is "actively considering" a rate cut at the June meeting, citing a meaningful softening in both CPI and PPI over the past two months. Core PCE for March came in at 2.1%, the closest reading to the 2% target in over two years.

Markets responded immediately. The S&P 500 surged 2.3% on the day, its best single-day gain since November 2024. The Nasdaq composite jumped 3.1%, led by rate-sensitive tech and growth names. The Russell 2000 small-cap index rallied 4.0%, historically the biggest beneficiary of rate cuts.

SECTOR BREAKOUT

Real estate (XLRE) exploded higher, up 4.8% — REITs reprice sharply when rate expectations shift dovish. Utilities (XLU) gained 3.2%. Technology (XLK) added 2.9%. Financials (XLF) were more muted at +1.1% as tighter net interest margins become a concern at lower rates.

WATCHLIST MOVERS

Tesla (TSLA) caught a bid on the growth rotation, rallying 5.6% — a Fed cut reduces the discount rate on future cash flows, benefiting high-multiple names. Palantir (PLTR) gained 7.2% as software multiples expanded. Google (GOOG) rose 2.8% on the combination of rate relief and strong ad revenue expectations into Q1 earnings next week.

BOND MARKET

The 2-year Treasury yield dropped 18 basis points to 4.01%, the sharpest single-day move since the SVB crisis. The 10-year fell to 4.14%. The yield curve steepened — a sign markets are pricing a soft landing over recession.

BOTTOM LINE

This is the clearest buying signal the market has sent in 18 months. The Fed is blinking. Growth assets, rate-sensitive sectors, and small-caps are all in play. Risk/reward has shifted meaningfully to the upside.
